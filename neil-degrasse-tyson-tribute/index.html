Neil DeGrasse Tyson

Neil DeGrasse Tyson

Your Personal Astrophysicist

Timeline

  • 1958 - Born in Manhattan, New York City
  • 1976 - Graduated from The Bronx High School of Science
  • 1980 - Graduated from Harvard College with an AB degree in Physics
  • 1986 - Became a lecturer in astronomy at the University of Maryland
  • 1988 - Accepted into the astronomy graduate program at Columbia University
  • 1989 - Earned an Masters in Astrophysics
  • 1991 - Earned a PhD degree in astrophysics
  • 1994 - Joined the Hayden Planetarium as a staff scientist while as a research affiliate with Princeton University
  • 1995 - Became acting director for Hayden Planetarium; began writing the "Universe" column for Natural History magazine
  • 1996 - Appointed director for Hayden Planetarium
  • 2000 - Honored as Sexiest Astrophysicist Alive, People Magazine
  • 2001 - Appointed to serve on the Commission on the Future of the United State Aerospace Industry; Awarded Medal of Excellence, Columbia University, New York City
  • 2004 - Served on the President's Commission on Implementation of United States Space Exploration Policy; hosted the PBS Nova miniseries; Awarded NASA Distinguished Public Service Medal
  • 2009 - Launched the StarTalk radio talk show; Awarded Douglas S. Morrow Public Outreach award from the Space Foundation for significant contributions to public awareness of space programs
  • 2014 - Helped revive Carl Sagan's television series, Cosmos: A Personal Voyage with his own, Cosmos: A Spacetime Odyssey; Awarded Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Reality Show Host; Awarded Dunlap Prize
  • 2015 - Began hosting a late-night talkshow, Star Talk on the National Geographic Channel; Awarded Hubbard Medal, National Geographic Society

Awards

  • 2001 - Medal of Excellence, Columbia University, New York City
  • 2005 - Science Writing Award
  • 2007 - Klopsteg Memorial Award winner
  • 2009 - Douglas S. Morrow Public Outreach Award from the Space Foundation for significant contributions to public awareness of space programs
  • 2009 - Isaac Asimov Award from the American Humanist Association
  • 2014 - Critics' Choice Television Award for Best Reality Show Host
  • 2014 - Awarded Dunlap Prize
  • 2015 - 2Public Welfare Medal from the National Academy of Sciences
  • 2015 - Cosmos Award, Planetary Society

Honors

  • 2000 - Sexiest Astrophysicist Alive, People Magazine
  • 2001 - asteroid named: 13123 Tyson, renamed from Asteroid 1994KA by the International Astronomical Union
  • 2001 - The Tech 100, voted by editors of Crain's Magazine to be among the 100 most influential technology leaders in New York
  • 2004 - Fifty Most Important African-Americans in Research Science
  • 2007 - Harvard 100: Most Influential Harvard Alumni Magazine, Cambridge, Massachusetts
  • 2007 - The Time 100, voted by the editors of Time Magazine as one of the 100 most influential persons in the world
  • 2008 - Discover Magazine selected him as one of "The 10 Most Influential People in Science"
